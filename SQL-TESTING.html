QA Excellence: HR Analytics Dashboard Testing

HR Analytics Dashboard Testing

Introduction:

A comprehensive project to create and analyze a Human Resources database using PostgreSQL. The primary goal was to perform Quality Assurance (QA) testing on the HR Analytics Dashboard developed in Tableau Desktop and Power BI. This involved functional and data validation, ensuring the accuracy and quality of data, and creating a detailed test document for comprehensive testing.

Goal:

The goal of this project was to create a robust HR Database, execute SQL queries, and meticulously QA test the HR Analytics Dashboard developed in Tableau and Power BI. The QA process involved functional validation, ensuring each feature adheres to requirements, and data validation by cross-referencing values between Tableau, Power BI reports, and SQL queries. A detailed test document was crafted, containing screenshots and queries used for thorough testing.

Outcome:

1. Employee Count:

  • The total employee count in the HR database is 1470.

  • In the 'High School' education category, there are 170 employees.

  • The 'Sales Executive' job role has an employee count of 91, and the 'Sales' department has 373 employees.

2. Attrition:

  • The overall attrition count is 9.

  • Among employees with a 'Doctoral Degree' in the 'R&D' department, there are 9 cases of attrition.

3. Attrition Rate:

  • The overall attrition rate is 16.12%.

  • In the 'Sales' department, the attrition rate is higher at 20.63%.

4. Active Employee:

  • The number of active employees is 1233.

  • The active employee count drops to 1173 when considering only male employees.

5. Average Age:

  • The average age of employees in the database is 37.

6. Attrition by Gender:

  • Among employees who experienced attrition, 150 are male, and 87 are female.

  • In the 'High School' education category, male attrition is higher than female attrition.

7. Department-wise Attrition:

  • In the 'R&D' department, 56.12% of attrition cases occur, followed by 'Sales' with 38.82%.

8. No of Employee by Age Group:

  • The age group '25 - 34' has the highest employee count, with 554 employees.

9. Education Field-wise Attrition:

  • Attrition is most prominent in 'Life Sciences' (89 cases) and least in 'Human Resources' (7 cases).

10. Attrition Rate by Gender for different Age Group:

  • In the age group '25 - 34,' male attrition is 29.11%, while female attrition is 18.14%.

11. Job Satisfaction Rating:

The job satisfaction rating is presented across job roles, revealing insights into employee satisfaction levels.
